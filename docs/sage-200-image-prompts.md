## 3 — Partnership ⚠ replace

**Ratio:** 4:3 · **File:** `sage-200-partnership.jpg`

Currently: eight people around a boardroom table, **all smiling straight at
the camera**, with a fabricated dashboard behind them showing dollar amounts.

This is the one stock cliché on the site, and it breaks two house rules at
once: everybody grinning at camera, and a plausible-but-fake financial
interface on screen. Dollar figures also read as imported on a Kenyan page.

**Replacement prompt:**

> Bright, warm corporate photograph, 35mm lens, f/2.8, generous daylight
> through a wall of windows. Five or six colleagues around a large light
> boardroom table in a modern Nairobi office, mid-meeting and clearly going
> well — one standing and presenting with an open gesture and a genuine
> smile, the others turned toward them, one laughing, one making a note. A
> mix of Black African and South Asian men and women, mid-30s to mid-50s,
> ordinary professional builds, smart business dress with some colour.
> **Nobody is looking at the camera.** On the table: notebooks, water glasses,
> a couple of laptops angled away with dim screens. Behind, a wall screen that
> is switched off or shows only an indistinct blur. Warm vivid grade, rich
> skin tones, clean bright whites.
>
> Avoid: anybody looking at or smiling at the camera, posed group line-ups,
> thumbs up, high fives, applause, handshakes, readable screen content, any
> visible dashboard, chart or financial interface, currency symbols, dark or
> grey light, desaturated grade, glass skyscrapers, identical dark suits,
> logos or branding on walls, screens, mugs or lanyards, wall banners or
> pull-up stands, over-smoothed skin, extra fingers.

**Why:** the section is "Working with WIZAG" — a working relationship, not a
company photo. The energy should be in the meeting, not aimed at the lens.
