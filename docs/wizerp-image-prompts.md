# WizERP — image generation prompts

Two images for `/erp/wizerp`.

> **⚠ This page uses a DIFFERENT visual register from the rest of the site.**
> WizCRM, TeamKazi and the Sage pages are shot in a restrained documentary
> style — muted, serious, understated. That was wrong for WizERP. This is
> WIZAG's own flagship, and it should look like **growth, momentum and
> success**, not like an audit.
>
> Earlier prompts here specified "competent and unglamorous", "no smiling"
> and a desaturated grade. That produced tired, joyless offices. Everything
> below is a deliberate reversal.

---

## Art direction — apply to both

| | |
|---|---|
| **Style** | Bright, aspirational corporate photography. The kind of image a successful company uses in its own annual report. |
| **Camera** | 35mm or 50mm, f/2.5, eye level, slightly low angle for a confident stance. |
| **Light** | **Abundant warm daylight.** Big windows, sun coming in, bright bounce. Golden and generous, never grey or flat. |
| **Grade** | **Warm and vivid.** Rich skin tones, clean whites, a little saturation. NOT desaturated, NOT muted, NOT moody. |
| **Setting** | A modern, well-appointed, clearly successful Nairobi office. Good furniture, plants, glass and light. A business that is visibly doing well. |
| **Mood** | **Energy, optimism, momentum.** People enjoying good work and winning at it. |

### The people

**A mix of Black African and South Asian professionals, men and women**,
**mid-30s to mid-50s**, **ordinary professional builds and faces rather than
fashion models**. Smart business dress with some colour — not a wall of dark
suits.

**Expressions: warm and genuinely happy.** Real smiles, animated
conversation, someone mid-laugh, leaning in, gesturing with energy. These are
people whose business is growing and who are pleased about it.

### The line between confident and cheesy

This is the whole craft of these two images, so be specific about both sides:

**Yes — authentic success**
- Genuine smiles that reach the eyes, arising from the conversation
- Animated gesture, leaning in, movement, people talking over each other
- Bright sunlight, warm colour, open modern space
- One person clearly making a point others are enjoying

**No — stock-photo cheese**
- Everybody lined up grinning straight at the camera
- Thumbs up, high fives, fists in the air, applause
- Confetti, champagne, trophies, cash, rising-arrow props
- Arms folded in a row, "power pose" line-ups
- Handshakes as the subject of the photo

The distinction is simple: **the happiness should come from what they are
doing, not from being photographed.**

## Image 1 — Growth, discussed

**Ratio:** 4:3 · **Filename:** `wizerp-growth.jpg`

Sits beside the Kenyan-compliance section, where the page talks about scoping
the system around the business.

> Bright, warm corporate photograph, 50mm lens, f/2.5, strong natural
> daylight flooding in from a large window. Three colleagues around the
> corner of a light timber table in a modern, well-appointed Nairobi office,
> in an animated and clearly enjoyable working discussion. A Black African
> woman in her forties in a well-cut jacket is making a point with an open
> gesture and a genuine smile; a South Asian man in his fifties in a crisp
> open-collar shirt is laughing and nodding as he listens; a younger Black
> African man leans in with a pen, engaged. Ordinary professional builds and
> faces, not fashion models. Nobody is looking at the camera. On the table: a
> printed report with clear columns but no legible words, an open laptop
> angled away with an indistinct screen, a notepad, coffee cups. Behind them
> a bright open office with plants, glass partitions and sunlight. Warm vivid
> grade, rich skin tones, clean whites, shallow depth of field. Optimistic
> and full of energy. Negative space upper right.
>
> Avoid: everyone grinning at camera, thumbs up, high fives, applause,
> handshakes as the subject, confetti or celebration props, cash or money,
> arms folded in a row, dark or grey lighting, desaturated or moody grade,
> cramped or dated offices, readable screen content, dashboards or
> spreadsheets legible on screen, legible figures on paper, logos or branding
> on walls, laptops, mugs, lanyards or notebooks, wall slogans or motivational
> posters, over-smoothed skin, extra fingers.

## Image 2 — The team that builds it

**Ratio:** 21:9 (wide letterbox band) · **Filename:** `wizerp-team.jpg`

⚠ **A wide band across the section, not a portrait slot.** Ask for **21:9**,
or generate 16:9 and crop — the composition must survive losing top and
bottom, so keep the group central with room either side.

> Bright, warm corporate photograph, 35mm lens, f/2.8, sunlight streaming
> through a wall of windows. Four colleagues of a software team gathered
> around a desk in a modern, bright Nairobi office, mid-discussion over an
> open laptop and a large printed process diagram. One is standing, gesturing
> at the diagram with energy and a broad genuine smile; the others are
> leaning in, one laughing, one pointing at something on the page. A mix of
> Black African and South Asian men and women, mid-30s to mid-50s, ordinary
> professional builds. Smart business dress with some colour — open-collar
> shirts, a bright blouse, one jacket, no ties. Nobody looking at camera; all
> the energy is in the work. The office around them is open, sunlit and
> well-appointed, with plants and glass. Warm vivid grade, rich skin tones,
> clean bright whites. A room where something is going well. Wide composition
> with generous space either side of the group.
>
> Avoid: posed group shots, everyone facing camera, thumbs up, high fives,
> applause, celebration props, handshakes as the subject, dark or grey
> lighting, desaturated grade, dated or cramped offices, exposed-brick
> startup clichés, readable screen content, legible text on the printed
> diagram, logos or branding on walls, laptops, mugs, lanyards or notebooks,
> wall banners or pull-up stands of any kind, over-smoothed skin, extra
> fingers.

**Why this shot:** the section's argument is that the people who wrote the
software are the people who deliver it. This is the only place those people
appear, so they need to look like a team you would want on your project —
capable and clearly enjoying it.

## Check every background surface

One Sage 200 image had to be cropped because a background banner read
**"Make an impact that matters"** — Deloitte's actual global tagline, and they
have a large Nairobi practice.

Generators reproduce real corporate slogans and near-miss logos from training
data, and place them exactly where a real office would: wall banners, pull-up
stands, glass partitions, laptop lids, mugs, lanyards, monitor bezels.

**Image 2 is the higher risk**, because a bright open-plan scene invites
exactly that kind of background dressing.

---

## Checking before use

- **Hands.** Count fingers on everyone, including anyone at frame edge.
  Gesturing hands are the highest-risk element in both of these.
- **Teeth.** These images have real smiles, which makes this the most likely
  failure. Generators fuse, multiply and misalign teeth.
- **Eyes.** Same direction, same size, catchlights on the same side.
- **Ears, glasses arms, collars, buttons.**
- **Every background surface**, per the note above.
- **Any incidental text.** Column and paragraph *shapes* are fine; readable
  words are not.
- **Screens.** If any screen shows a plausible ERP or accounting interface,
  regenerate — this page's readers use those daily.

If a frame is right in every way except one hand or one smile, regenerate
rather than retouch.

**Generate each image on its own, not as a 2×2 grid.** A single 1536px image
gives roughly double the usable resolution of one panel and needs no cropping.

---

## If the first attempts still come back flat

Two adjustments usually fix it, in this order:

1. **Push the light.** Add "golden hour sunlight streaming through large
   windows, bright and airy, high-key lighting" to the front of the prompt.
   Dull light is what makes an office look like a waiting room.
2. **Name the emotion in the action.** Instead of "smiling", write what causes
   it: "laughing at something a colleague just said", "delighted by a result
   on the page". Generators render a described *moment* far better than a
   described *expression*.

## Dropping them in

Save to `public/images/wizerp/`, then in `src/pages/erp/wizerp.astro` replace
each block:

```astro
<!-- before -->
<ImageSlot ratio="4/3" brief="..." purpose="..." />

<!-- after -->
<img
  src="/images/wizerp/wizerp-growth.jpg"
  alt="Three colleagues in animated discussion around a table in a sunlit office."
  width="1160"
  height="870"
  loading="lazy"
  decoding="async"
  class="w-full rounded-[var(--radius-md)] object-cover"
/>
```

Write a real `alt` for each — describe what is in the frame, not the
marketing intent. Then remove the now-unused `ImageSlot` import.

Confirm nothing is left behind with:

```
Get-ChildItem -Path src -Recurse -Include *.astro | Select-String -Pattern "<ImageSlot"
```
