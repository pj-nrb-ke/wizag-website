# Industries — image prompts

**Read `docs/photography-direction.md` first** for the house style (bright
warm daylight, faces engaged with the work and never the camera, no readable
screens, no logos or wall slogans). Prompts below are paste-ready.

Nine images, one per sector page. Each sector page already renders a
documented placeholder from `image.brief` in `src/data/industries.ts`; drop
the generated file into `public/images/industries/` and add `file`/`alt`/`w`/
`h` to that sector's `image` object to swap it in.

## The one rule that matters most here

**Every one of these buyers is a decision maker who will recognise a fake
instantly.** More than any other section, the photography has to read as a
real Kenyan workplace of that specific kind — not an imported stock scene.

Three sectors also carry a hard content rule, because getting it wrong is
worse than having no image at all:

- **Healthcare** — no patients, no clinical procedures, no wards. The buyer
  is hospital *administration*. Photograph the finance and admin office.
- **NGOs** — no beneficiaries, no field photography, no donor logos. The
  buyer is the *finance* function. Photograph the finance office.
- **Public sector** — no flags, crests, national insignia or identifiable
  government buildings. Any of those implies an official endorsement that
  does not exist.

All are **4:3** unless noted. Generate one at a time, not as a grid.

## 1. `manufacturing-industrial.jpg`

```
Bright, warm corporate photograph, 35mm lens, f/2.8, abundant daylight. Two colleagues on a clean, well-lit, orderly production floor of a modern Kenyan manufacturing plant — one holding a tablet, the other pointing at a rack of stock or a machine, both engaged and one smiling. Mid-30s to mid-50s, a mix of Black African and South Asian, smart work shirts. Faces visible, neither looking at camera. Busy, prosperous, in control. Warm vivid grade, rich skin tones, clean light from high windows.

Avoid: single robot arm as the subject, dark dramatic factory lighting, sparks and welding-mask clichés, derelict or dirty plant, hi-vis fashion shoot, readable screens, branded packaging or product logos, grinning at camera, desaturated grade, extra fingers.
```

## Dropping them in

Save to `public/images/industries/`, then in `src/data/industries.ts` add to
the relevant sector's `image` object:

```ts
image: {
  file: 'manufacturing-industrial.jpg',
  alt: 'Two colleagues on a production floor, one holding a tablet, the other pointing at a stock rack.',
  w: 1160,
  h: 870,
  brief: '…',      // keep the brief/purpose; they document the shot
  purpose: '…',
},
```

The template shows the `<img>` automatically once `file` is present, and
falls back to the documented placeholder until then. Write a real `alt`
describing what is in the frame, not the marketing intent.

Confirm none are left behind before launch with:

```
Get-ChildItem -Path src -Recurse -Include *.astro | Select-String -Pattern "ImageSlot"
```
