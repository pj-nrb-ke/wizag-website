## What this product is

A managed ERP platform sold to **accounting practices**, who run their own SME
clients on it. The people in frame are **practice principals** — the owners of
the firm, not their staff. The register is two partners who have just realised
this solves a problem they have been carrying.

## 1 — Two principals, one decision

**Ratio:** 4:3 · **File:** `ascendbooks-partners.jpg`

> Bright, warm corporate photograph, 50mm lens, f/2.5, strong daylight from a
> large window. Two senior partners of an accounting practice at the corner of
> a light meeting table in a modern, well-appointed Nairobi office, in an
> animated and clearly positive conversation. A Black African woman in her
> forties in a well-cut jacket is holding a tablet angled between them and
> making a point with an open gesture and a genuine smile; a South Asian man
> in his fifties in a crisp open-collar shirt is smiling and nodding as he
> follows — they have found something that works. Ordinary professional builds
> and faces, not fashion models. Faces visible, neither looking at camera. On
> the table: the tablet with a dim indistinct screen, a notepad and pen, two
> coffee cups. Behind them a bright office with plants, glass and sunlight.
> Warm vivid grade, rich skin tones, clean whites, shallow depth of field.
> Negative space upper right.
>
> Avoid: grinning at camera, handshakes, thumbs up, high fives, applause,
> celebration props, laughing at a screen, arms folded in a row, identical
> dark suits, glass skyscrapers, open-plan startup clichés, readable screen
> content, dashboards or spreadsheets legible on screen, legible figures on
> paper, dark or grey light, desaturated grade, logos or branding on walls,
> laptops, mugs, notebooks or lanyards, wall slogans, over-smoothed skin,
> extra fingers.

**Why:** everything else on the page is a diagram or a list. The one
photograph should carry what diagrams cannot — that this is a commercial
decision made by two people who own the firm, and they are pleased with it.
